NEON RISING

Game Overview

Neon Rising is a dynamic vertical platformer developed in Unity using C#. It features a complete narrative in which the protagonist must ascend to confront the person who murdered their father in childhood. The player uses a bow not only as a weapon but also as a teleportation tool, allowing creative mobility through levels filled with enemies and platform challenges. Almost all assets, from sprites to background environments and interface elements, were handcrafted by me.

Technology Stack

  • Engine: Unity
  • Language: C#
  • Tools: Unity Editor (no external plugins)
  • Managers Implemented: Game Manager, Audio Manager, UI Manager, Level Manager, Random Manager

Core Architecture

The game is structured around a modular manager-based architecture to handle core systems such as audio, UI, level control, and procedural content. The game contains two fixed levels (tutorial and a main level) designed manually for precision gameplay and story pacing. In addition, there is a fully procedural game mode where platforms and enemy configurations are generated dynamically. Game logic is component-driven and follows clear separation of concerns between player input, physics, collision, and UI.

Game Loop & Progression

The player progresses through a vertically scrolling level, evading or defeating enemies using arrow types. The camera scrolls upward at a constant rate, forcing the player to ascend continuously. If the player falls below the camera view or touches a lethal trap/enemy, the level resets. The game begins with a tutorial level, followed by the main fixed level which concludes in a boss battle. For extended gameplay and replayability, a procedural mode offers new challenges in each session. A full run typically lasts between 20 and 45 minutes.

Development Context

This was my first full game development project, carried out as part of a university course. The team originally consisted of six people, but after the first week one member left the degree, and the workload was redistributed among the remaining five. I was responsible for the procedural level generation system and the complete functionality of the platforming mechanics. In addition, I created most of the visual assets, including characters, interface elements, and background environments.

This experience was a turning point for me. I learned core programming practices in C#, how to manage partial team loss under time pressure, and how to adapt iteratively while delivering a functional game. It also taught me the importance of structuring game logic modularly and anticipating edge cases when designing player interaction systems.

Guerrilla Showcase

In 2023, we showcased Neon Rising at the Guerrilla event hosted at our university. The game received an excellent reception from attendees, and we gathered insightful feedback from players and professionals alike. It was a motivating experience that highlighted the strengths of our design and helped us reflect on areas for improvement.

Telemetry & Game Analytics

We are currently collaborating with a new group to develop a telemetry system for games. This system is already being integrated into Neon Rising to monitor metrics such as level complexity, player behavior, and the effectiveness of enemy types. The data allows us to balance gameplay, refine enemy placement, and ensure difficulty scales appropriately. It's a practical tool that enhances the development process through measurable insights.
